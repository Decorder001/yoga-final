Welcome to the Beginner Yoga Series

Our beginner series is designed to help you build a solid foundation for your yoga practice. Whether you are new to yoga or looking to revisit the basics, this series will guide you through simple postures and techniques that will prepare you for more advanced practices.

The highlight of our beginner series is the Surya Namaskar (Sun Salutation), a powerful sequence of 12 poses that stretches and strengthens the body while connecting the mind and breath. Below, you'll find each step of Surya Namaskar, along with its corresponding mantra and benefits.

1. Pranamasana (Prayer Pose)

Om Mitraya Namah

Start by standing straight, feet together, and palms in prayer position. This pose helps in centering your body and mind.

2. Hastauttanasana (Raised Arms Pose)

Om Ravaye Namah

Raise your arms above your head, arching your back slightly. This pose stretches the chest and abdomen, improving digestion.

3. Hastapadasana (Standing Forward Bend)

Om Suryaya Namah

Bend forward, trying to touch the floor with your hands. This pose stretches the hamstrings and strengthens the spine.

4. Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Equestrian Pose)

Om Bhanave Namah

Step your right leg back and bring your left knee forward. This pose strengthens the legs and increases flexibility in the hips.

5. Dandasana (Stick Pose)

Om Khagaya Namah

Step your left leg back to come into a plank position. This pose strengthens the arms, wrists, and core.

6. Ashtanga Namaskara (Eight Limbed Pose)

Om Pushne Namah

Lower your knees, chest, and chin to the ground while keeping your hips lifted. This pose improves flexibility and strengthens the spine.

7. Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose)

Om Hiranyagarbhaya Namah

Lift your chest up while keeping your elbows bent and your legs extended. This pose strengthens the back and opens the chest.

8. Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward Facing Dog Pose)

Om Marichaye Namah

Lift your hips up and straighten your legs, forming an inverted V shape. This pose stretches the hamstrings and strengthens the arms.

9. Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Equestrian Pose)

Om Adityaya Namah

Bring your right leg forward between your hands, keeping your left leg extended. This pose strengthens the legs and improves flexibility in the hips.

10. Hastapadasana (Standing Forward Bend)

Om Savitre Namah

Bring your feet together and bend forward, trying to touch the floor. This pose stretches the hamstrings and lengthens the spine.

11. Hastauttanasana (Raised Arms Pose)

Om Arkaya Namah

Raise your arms above your head and arch your back slightly. This pose stretches the chest and abdomen, promoting flexibility.

12. Pranamasana (Prayer Pose)

Om Bhaskaraya Namah

Return to a standing position with your palms together in prayer. This pose brings calmness and mental focus.

Benefits of Surya Namaskar

  • Improves flexibility and tones muscles.
  • Boosts circulation and stimulates the cardiovascular system.
  • Helps in weight loss by burning calories and improving metabolism.
  • Calms the nervous system, reducing stress and anxiety.
  • Enhances lung capacity, leading to better breathing.
  • Promotes overall mental clarity and focus.
